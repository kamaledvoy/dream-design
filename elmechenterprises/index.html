Solar Energy

Solar energy, widely recognised as a cost-competitive and reliable source of energy, has come a long way in a decade. The revolution began in 2008 when there were 6.7 GW worth new installations from 2.5 GW the year before. Back then, the global market was highly dependent on subsidy regimes as solar energy harvesting was expensive and highly inefficient which is now increasingly low cost. In 2020, there will be more than 115 GW of solar installed world over, according to the World Economic Forum.

The wonders of technology are not hidden, and it seems that it has caught up solar energy as well, in terms of cost and efficiency. In fact, in the coming years, technological advancements will ensure that solar becomes even cheaper.
